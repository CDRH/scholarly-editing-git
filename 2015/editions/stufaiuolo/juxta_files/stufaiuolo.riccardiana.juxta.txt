       Written in 1559 by Doni after he left Venice, probably while living in Ancona or in
        Ferrara, as per date written on the frontispisce f. 2r (the text itself was
        composed ante 1551). The dedicatee is Giacomo Piccolomini duke of Montemarciano, but he
        probably lived in Siena, where his son Alfonso is reported to have born. reagion.
       The ownership note at f. 2r declares that the manuscript was owned
        by Raffaello di Lionardo Carnesecchi (1547-1621). It is not clear how the manuscript got in
        the possession of Carnesecch; the possession of Alfonso Piccolomini where confiscated by the
        Pope (in Montemarciano) and the granduke of Tuscany in Siena, so it si possible that the
        accession of the Carnessecchi was a consequence of the dispersion of the Piccolomini estate
        after teh execution of Alfonso (1591). Carnesecchi died in 1615.
       Next we know the manuscript having been possessed by Anton Maria Salvini, who
        died in 1729. After his death the heairs negotiated the sale of his library to Gabriello
        Riccardi. The shelfmark 1184is the original shelfmark attributed by Gabriello. 
        It is not very clear how the Stufaiuolo ended up consociated with the other
        items that compose the miscellaneous and way it was originally coolcated toward the end of
        such miscellanea, since the shelfmark was not changed. The miscellanea assumes its present
        configuarion in 1858 when the pieces get bound by XXX

     Tutti fuori

        prego
